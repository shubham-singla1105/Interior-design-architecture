Assess the damage: Start by assessing the extent of the damage to your interior. This will help you determine whether the damage is cosmetic or structural, and what repairs will be necessary.
Determine the cause: Once you know the extent of the damage, try to determine what caused it. This will help you prevent similar damage from occurring in the future.
Contact a professional: Depending on the severity of the damage, you may need to contact a professional for repairs. Consider hiring a reputable contractor or interior designer who specializes in the type of repairs you need.

A robust building is a structure that is designed to withstand a variety of hazards, including natural disasters, extreme weather events, and human-caused hazards.
Structural integrity: A robust building is built with strong and durable materials that can withstand high winds, earthquakes, and other natural disasters.
Redundancy: A robust building may have redundant systems, such as backup generators or multiple water sources, to ensure that essential services remain available in the event of a failure.
Resistance to fire: A robust building is designed with fire-resistant materials and may have fire suppression systems, such as sprinklers or fire extinguishers, to minimize the risk of fire damage.

Design consultation: This involves working with clients to understand their needs, preferences, and budget, and developing a design plan that meets their requirements.
Space planning: This involves analyzing the available space and developing a layout plan that optimizes the functionality and flow of the space.
Material and finish selection: This involves selecting materials, finishes, and colors that align with the design plan and create a cohesive and aesthetically pleasing look.
Furniture selection: This involves selecting furniture pieces that are functional, comfortable, and visually appealing, and that complement the overall design scheme.
Lighting design: This involves selecting and positioning light fixtures to create a well-lit and inviting space, while also highlighting key features and architectural elements.
Project management: This involves overseeing the entire design process, from initial consultation to final installation, and ensuring that the project is completed on time and within budget.
Overall, the main services of interior architecture and design are aimed at creating functional, aesthetically pleasing, and comfortable spaces that meet the needs and preferences of the clients.
